
BANK ACCOUNT ROLE & OWNERSHIP CHANGE PROCEDURE
Test Data Procedural Document (Human-Oriented)
Document Code: BANK-ACCT-ROLES-OWNERSHIP-PROC-2026-02
Version: 1.0
Effective Date: 2026-02-26
Owner Function: Retail & Business Banking Operations
Applies To: Personal, Savings, Corporate accounts

0.0 DOCUMENT CONTROL
0.1. This document is provided as test data and illustrates typical bank operational controls.
0.2. Where local law or policy conflicts with this document, the stricter control applies.
0.3. All staff must follow the bank’s internal AML/KYC, sanctions, fraud, and data protection policies when executing any step described herein.

1.0 PURPOSE, SCOPE, AND PRINCIPLES
1.1. Purpose: Define standardized requirements and step-by-step procedures for changing account ownership and/or role assignments on bank accounts.
1.2. Scope: This procedure applies to (a) changing the Owner, (b) adding/removing Admins, and (c) adding/removing Named users, for Personal, Savings, and Corporate accounts.
1.3. Out of Scope: Changes to account product type, interest rate, pricing, or overdraft terms are not governed by this document.
1.4. Principle of Least Privilege: Access provided to any role must be the minimum required for the stated need.
1.5. Segregation of Duties: No single staff member may both (a) approve identity verification exceptions and (b) finalize the role/ownership change in core banking systems.
1.6. Non-Repudiation: All approvals must be captured in a durable audit trail linked to the customer, account, request, and staff user IDs.
1.7. Customer Safety: Where suspected coercion, fraud, or financial abuse exists, changes must be paused and escalated per fraud/abuse procedures.

2.0 DEFINITIONS AND ROLE MODEL
2.1. Account: A deposit account maintained by the bank, including Personal, Savings, and Corporate accounts.
2.2. Ownership (Owner Role): The legal authority over the account. “Owner” means the person/entity with ultimate authority to control the account, subject to law and bank policy.
2.3. Admin Role: A delegated authority role intended for operational management without conferring ultimate legal ownership.
2.4. Named Role: A constrained role used for limited access (view, statements, or restricted transaction permissions if permitted).
2.5. Primary Authorized Signatory (Corporate): The individual acting on behalf of the corporate Owner entity under the account mandate.
2.6. Mandate (Corporate): The bank-held instruction set defining who can operate the corporate account and with what approval rules.
2.7. Beneficial Owner (Corporate): A natural person who ultimately owns/controls the corporate entity as defined by the bank’s AML program.
2.8. Request: A documented instruction to add, remove, or modify a role or ownership status.
2.9. Verification: The bank’s process of confirming identity, authority, and legitimacy of the request.

3.0 ACCOUNT TYPES COVERED
3.1. Personal Account: Held by one or more individuals for personal use.
3.2. Savings Account: An interest-bearing account with heightened protective controls due to long-term asset purpose and typical withdrawal restrictions.
3.3. Corporate Account: Held by a registered business entity; operation is governed by mandate, signatories, corporate authority documents, and beneficial ownership controls.

4.0 AUTHORITY AND PERMISSION MODEL (ROLE CAPABILITIES)
4.1. Owner Permissions (all account types):
4.1.1. May authorize addition/removal of Admins and Named users (subject to account type rules).
4.1.2. May change account operating instructions (e.g., signing rules) where applicable.
4.1.3. May authorize account closure (subject to holds, liens, legal blocks, and debt recovery).
4.1.4. May request ownership change only where ownership change is legally and product-policy permitted.
4.2. Admin Permissions (default model; may be reduced by configuration):
4.2.1. May initiate/authorize routine transactions within assigned limits.
4.2.2. May add/remove Named users only if explicitly granted in the account configuration and permitted for the account type.
4.2.3. Must not be permitted to change Owner or corporate mandate by default.
4.2.4. Must not be permitted to remove the Owner or lock the Owner out of access.
4.3. Named Permissions (default model; may be reduced further):
4.3.1. May access statements and account information only to the extent configured.
4.3.2. May be granted limited transactional permissions only where allowed by account type and where limits are explicitly set.
4.3.3. Must not be permitted to add/remove users, modify roles, or change account controls.
4.4. Staff Permissions (internal):
4.4.1. Frontline staff may accept requests and capture documents.
4.4.2. Operations staff may execute changes in systems following verification and approvals.
4.4.3. Compliance staff may block or delay changes where AML/KYC, sanctions, or suspicious activity concerns arise.

5.0 GLOBAL REQUIREMENTS FOR ANY ROLE OR OWNERSHIP CHANGE
5.1. Verified Request Source: The request must come from a party with authority to instruct the bank (see Section 6.0 and account-type rules).
5.2. Identity Verification (KYC): Any person being added or elevated (e.g., Named → Admin, Admin → Owner) must pass identity verification prior to activation.
5.3. Authority Verification: The bank must confirm the requester has authority for the requested change (e.g., Owner consent, PoA, executor, corporate resolution).
5.4. Sanctions/Watchlist Screening: All newly added individuals and relevant corporate parties must be screened per bank policy.
5.5. Fraud and Coercion Checks: Staff must assess red flags including unusual urgency, inconsistent instructions, third-party pressure, or abrupt changes in control.
5.6. Cooling-Off (where configured): For high-risk changes (e.g., Owner change, new Admin with transaction powers), the bank may apply a cooling-off period before enabling outbound payments.
5.7. Account Blocks and Holds: Changes must not be executed if the account is under legal hold, court order, freeze, bankruptcy/insolvency block, or internal investigation, unless authorized by Legal/Compliance.
5.8. Multi-Channel Consistency: Requests received digitally must be re-validated for strong customer authentication; requests received in person must include document and signature controls.
5.9. Record Retention: All documents, verification results, and approvals must be retained per the bank’s retention schedule (minimum 7 years unless longer is required).
5.10. Audit Trail Fields: Each change record must capture account number, change type, old roles, new roles, effective date/time, authorizing party, channel, staff ID(s), and reason code.
5.11. Notification: The bank must notify impacted parties per policy (e.g., current Owner, added Admin), except where notification is restricted by legal/compliance instruction.

6.0 WHO MAY REQUEST CHANGES (REQUESTER ELIGIBILITY)
6.1. Owner-Initiated Requests: The current Owner may request any permitted role change, subject to verification.
6.2. Admin-Initiated Requests: An Admin may request only changes explicitly permitted to Admins (typically add/remove Named users), subject to configured authority.
6.3. Named-Initiated Requests: A Named user may not request ownership or role changes.
6.4. Legal Representative Requests: A verified attorney-in-fact, guardian, executor, or court-appointed representative may request changes within the scope of their authority documents.
6.5. Corporate Authority Requests: For Corporate accounts, only individuals authorized under the mandate and corporate authority documents may request changes.
6.6. Staff-Initiated Changes: Staff must not initiate role changes except to correct operational errors, and only with documented supervisory approval.
6.7. Third-Party Requests: Requests from unrelated third parties must be rejected unless accompanied by verified legal authority.

7.0 CHANGE TYPES AND PROCESS OVERVIEW
7.1. Change Type A: Add Role (add Owner where permitted, add Admin, add Named).
7.2. Change Type B: Remove Role (remove Admin, remove Named, remove Owner where permitted and only under defined scenarios).
7.3. Change Type C: Modify Role (change limits, change access scope, elevate or downgrade role).
7.4. Change Type D: Transfer Ownership (rare; only permitted for defined circumstances).
7.5. Standard Processing Stages:
7.5.1. Intake and triage.
7.5.2. Document collection.
7.5.3. Identity verification.
7.5.4. Authority verification.
7.5.5. Risk checks (AML/sanctions/fraud).
7.5.6. Approvals.
7.5.7. System execution.
7.5.8. Confirmation and notification.
7.5.9. Post-change monitoring and case closure.

8.0 INTAKE AND TRIAGE PROCEDURE (ALL ACCOUNT TYPES)
8.1. Create a Case: Log the request in the case management system with a unique reference ID.
8.2. Identify Account Type: Determine whether the account is Personal, Savings, or Corporate.
8.3. Identify Change Type: Classify as A/B/C/D (Section 7.0) and capture requested effective date.
8.4. Identify Parties: Record requester, affected account holders/users, and any representative/agent.
8.5. Initial Eligibility Check:
8.5.1. Confirm requester eligibility per Section 6.0.
8.5.2. Confirm the change is permitted for the account type (Sections 10.0–12.0).
8.6. Immediate Red Flags:
8.6.1. If suspected coercion, fraud, or abuse is present, pause processing and escalate.
8.6.2. If a legal hold or freeze exists, route to Legal/Compliance.
8.7. Provide Requirements Checklist: Provide the requester with the precise documentation requirements for the specific change.

9.0 VERIFICATION REQUIREMENTS (IDENTITY + AUTHORITY)
9.1. Identity Verification Methods:
9.1.1. In-person verification using government-issued photo ID and liveness checks (where used).
9.1.2. Remote verification using bank-approved digital identity tools and strong customer authentication.
9.1.3. Enhanced verification for high-risk cases (e.g., large balances, new high-privilege Admins, unusual jurisdictions).
9.2. Signature Verification:
9.2.1. Where wet signatures are used, compare to specimen signature on file.
9.2.2. Where e-signatures are used, ensure audit evidence of authentication and consent is retained.
9.3. Authority Documents (examples; exact list governed by policy):
9.3.1. Power of Attorney / attorney-in-fact documents.
9.3.2. Court orders, guardianship orders.
9.3.3. Probate documents and executor appointment.
9.3.4. Corporate resolutions, certificate of incumbency, registry extracts.
9.4. Validation of Authority Documents:
9.4.1. Confirm documents are current, unrevoked, and applicable to the account and requested action.
9.4.2. Confirm the scope explicitly includes authority to manage bank accounts and assign/remove access.
9.4.3. If scope is ambiguous, refer to Legal for interpretation before proceeding.
9.5. Beneficial Ownership (Corporate):
9.5.1. If the change impacts control or signatory authority, refresh beneficial ownership information per AML policy.
9.5.2. If beneficial ownership cannot be confirmed, do not enable high-privilege access.

10.0 PERSONAL ACCOUNT RULES (ROLE AND OWNERSHIP CHANGES)
10.1. Personal Ownership Change Restriction: Ownership transfer between living individuals is generally not permitted; instead, open a new account and transfer funds, unless a defined legal exception applies.
10.2. Permitted Ownership Change Scenarios (Personal):
10.2.1. Death of Owner: Transfer control to executor/administrator of estate upon verified probate authority.
10.2.2. Court Order: Implement ownership/control changes only as specified by a valid court order.
10.2.3. Legal Incapacity: Implement control changes only to a verified legal representative (e.g., attorney-in-fact/guardian) within document scope.
10.3. Adding an Admin (Personal):
10.3.1. Requires request by the Owner or verified legal representative.
10.3.2. Admin must pass identity verification and screening.
10.3.3. Admin permissions must be explicitly configured (e.g., payments enabled/disabled, limits).
10.4. Removing an Admin (Personal):
10.4.1. May be requested by Owner at any time.
10.4.2. Must be executed immediately if the Owner reports misuse or suspected compromise.
10.5. Adding a Named User (Personal):
10.5.1. May be requested by Owner (or Admin if explicitly permitted).
10.5.2. Named access must be limited to defined needs; view-only is preferred.
10.6. Removing a Named User (Personal):
10.6.1. May be requested by Owner or Admin (if permitted).
10.6.2. Must be executed immediately if access is no longer required.
10.7. Joint/Multiple Owners (if product supports it):
10.7.1. If multiple Owners exist, the account mandate defines whether one Owner can add/remove Admins/Named users or whether all Owners must consent.
10.7.2. If consent of all Owners is required, staff must obtain and verify consent from each Owner prior to execution.
10.8. Disputes Between Owners (Personal):
10.8.1. If conflicting instructions are received from Owners, freeze role changes and escalate to Legal/Compliance.
10.8.2. Where appropriate, restrict outbound transfers until resolution.

11.0 SAVINGS ACCOUNT RULES (ROLE AND OWNERSHIP CHANGES)
11.1. Savings Ownership Controls: Ownership transfer is restricted and generally follows the same rule as Personal accounts, with heightened scrutiny due to asset-protection purpose.
11.2. Named Access on Savings Accounts:
11.2.1. Default Named permission is view-only (statements and balance).
11.2.2. Transactional permissions for Named users are prohibited unless a specific product feature explicitly supports it and Compliance approves.
11.3. Admin Access on Savings Accounts:
11.3.1. Admin roles are discouraged and must be justified (e.g., caregiver support with verified authority).
11.3.2. If Admin is granted, payments and withdrawals must be limited and may be subject to enhanced monitoring.
11.3.3. A risk acknowledgment must be captured from the Owner prior to enabling Admin transactional access.
11.4. Ownership Change Scenarios (Savings):
11.4.1. Death of Owner: transfer control only with verified estate authority.
11.4.2. Legal incapacity: grant control only to verified legal representative.
11.4.3. Court order: implement strictly per order.
11.5. Withdrawal Restriction Integrity:
11.5.1. Role changes must not be used to bypass withdrawal limits or notice requirements.
11.5.2. If the change coincides with large withdrawals or closure, apply enhanced fraud/AML review.

12.0 CORPORATE ACCOUNT RULES (ROLE AND OWNERSHIP CHANGES)
12.1. Corporate “Owner” Definition: The Owner is the corporate entity; individuals act as authorized signatories and administrators under the mandate.
12.2. Change of Primary Authorized Signatory:
12.2.1. Requires updated corporate authority documentation (e.g., board resolution) specifying the appointment/removal.
12.2.2. Requires identity verification and screening of the new signatory.
12.2.3. Requires confirmation that the signatory change is consistent with the mandate rules (e.g., dual authorization thresholds).
12.3. Adding or Removing Admins (Corporate):
12.3.1. Must be requested by an authorized signatory per the current mandate.
12.3.2. Must be supported by a mandate update instruction if the Admin role affects signing powers.
12.3.3. Must define transaction limits, payment types enabled, and any dual-control requirements.
12.4. Adding or Removing Named Users (Corporate):
12.4.1. May be requested by authorized signatory or Admin if explicitly permitted.
12.4.2. Named users should not be granted approval powers for high-value payments unless dual control is in place.
12.5. Corporate Ownership/Control Changes (Entity-Level):
12.5.1. If the corporate entity undergoes merger, acquisition, or control change impacting beneficial ownership, the bank must refresh AML/KYC and beneficial ownership records.
12.5.2. Where control change is material, the bank may require re-papering of the mandate and re-approval of all signatories.
12.6. Corporate Insolvency and Administration:
12.6.1. Upon notification of insolvency, administration, or liquidation, suspend Admin and Named access unless the appointed administrator/liquidator authorizes otherwise.
12.6.2. Accept instructions only from verified insolvency practitioners within the scope of appointment.
12.7. Conflicting Corporate Instructions:
12.7.1. If conflicting instructions are received from directors or signatories, freeze role/mandate changes pending a clarified resolution or court order.

13.0 REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION BY CHANGE TYPE
13.1. Add Admin (Personal/Savings):
13.1.1. Owner instruction (signed in-channel).
13.1.2. Admin identity verification documents.
13.1.3. Explicit permission set and limits.
13.2. Add Named (Personal/Savings):
13.2.1. Owner/Admin instruction (as permitted).
13.2.2. Named identity verification proportional to access (view-only may require reduced KYC per policy; transactional access requires full verification).
13.3. Corporate Signatory/Admin changes:
13.3.1. Mandate update form/instruction.
13.3.2. Board resolution or equivalent authority evidence where required.
13.3.3. Identity verification and screening for all new individuals.
13.3.4. Updated beneficial ownership information when control changes.
13.4. Death of Owner (Personal/Savings):
13.4.1. Certified death certificate (or bank-approved verification alternative).
13.4.2. Probate/letters of administration/executor appointment.
13.4.3. Identity verification of executor/administrator.
13.4.4. Estate account opening documentation if funds are to be moved.
13.5. Incapacity / Power of Attorney:
13.5.1. Valid PoA/guardianship documents.
13.5.2. Identity verification of attorney-in-fact/guardian.
13.5.3. Legal review if scope is unclear or contested.
13.6. Court Orders:
13.6.1. Original or certified copy of order.
13.6.2. Legal department validation prior to execution.

14.0 EXECUTION CONTROLS (SYSTEM CHANGES)
14.1. Dual Control for High-Risk Changes:
14.1.1. Owner change, corporate mandate change, and enabling new payment privileges require maker-checker controls.
14.1.2. The maker and checker must be different staff members.
14.2. Effective Timing:
14.2.1. Changes should be applied with an effective timestamp and communicated to the customer.
14.2.2. Where cooling-off is required, set payment enablement to begin only after the cooling-off expires.
14.3. Access Provisioning:
14.3.1. Issue credentials only after verification completion.
14.3.2. Ensure multi-factor authentication enrollment for Admins and Owners where available.
14.4. Limit Configuration:
14.4.1. Set transaction types and limits explicitly (daily limits, per-transaction limits, payee controls).
14.4.2. For corporate accounts, enforce dual authorization thresholds as defined in the mandate.
14.5. Removal and Revocation:
14.5.1. Removing a role must immediately revoke digital access tokens, cards, and delegated authorities.
14.5.2. If cards are associated with Named/Admin access, block cards immediately upon removal.
14.6. Post-Change Review:
14.6.1. Confirm role listing in core systems matches the request.
14.6.2. Confirm online/mobile access reflects intended permissions.
14.6.3. Confirm notifications were sent and recorded.

15.0 RISK, COMPLIANCE, AND ESCALATION RULES
15.1. AML/Suspicious Activity:
15.1.1. If the change appears to facilitate unusual movement of funds, escalate to AML investigations.
15.1.2. Do not disclose investigation status to the customer beyond approved scripts.
15.2. Sanctions Screening Hits:
15.2.1. If screening returns a potential match, do not enable access until cleared.
15.2.2. Follow sanctions escalation and hold procedures.
15.3. Fraud Indicators:
15.3.1. If account takeover is suspected, immediately lock sensitive changes and escalate.
15.3.2. Consider step-up verification and out-of-band confirmation to the known Owner.
15.4. Vulnerable Customers / Financial Abuse:
15.4.1. If staff suspect coercion or abuse, pause role changes and follow safeguarding procedures.
15.5. Data Protection:
15.5.1. Only share account details with individuals who have verified authority.
15.5.2. Keep documents in approved systems with access logging.

16.0 DISPUTES AND CONFLICTING INSTRUCTIONS
16.1. Conflicting Requests: If two parties with apparent authority provide conflicting instructions, suspend changes until authority is clarified.
16.2. Alleged Unauthorized Admin/Named Additions:
16.2.1. Immediately suspend the disputed role’s access.
16.2.2. Re-verify the change authorization and signature evidence.
16.3. Claims of Forged Documents:
16.3.1. Escalate to Fraud and Legal.
16.3.2. Preserve all evidence and audit logs.
16.4. Court Injunctions or Restraining Orders:
16.4.1. Apply blocks exactly as directed and record the legal basis.

17.0 SERVICE LEVEL TARGETS AND CUSTOMER COMMUNICATION
17.1. Standard SLA: Complete role changes within 2 business days after receiving complete, verified documentation.
17.2. High-Risk/Legal SLA: Where Legal/Compliance review is required, provide an estimated timeline and status updates per communication policy.
17.3. Confirmation Notice Content:
17.3.1. What changed (role/permissions).
17.3.2. When it became effective.
17.3.3. How to report unauthorized changes.

18.0 EXAMPLES (FOR TRAINING / TEST DATA)
18.1. Example A (Personal → Add Admin): Owner requests adding spouse as Admin with $500 daily payment limit; Admin passes ID verification; maker-checker applied; notifications sent to Owner.
18.2. Example B (Savings → Add Named View-Only): Owner adds accountant as Named view-only for statements; no payment powers enabled; access reviewed after 90 days.
18.3. Example C (Corporate → Change Primary Signatory): Company provides board resolution appointing new CFO as signatory; KYC completed; mandate updated to require dual authorization over set threshold; prior CFO removed.
18.4. Example D (Personal → Death of Owner): Executor provides probate appointment; account is restricted; executor granted estate administration access; funds transferred to estate account.
