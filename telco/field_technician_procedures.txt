Field Technician Procedures & Best Practices
Document Version: 1.0
Effective Date: 2026-04-19
============================================================

SECTION 1: PRE-SITE VISIT PREPARATION
--------------------------------------------------------------

1.1  Confirm work order details with dispatch before departing depot, including
     site address, service type (fibre/copper/wireless), and customer contact.

1.2  Verify that your vehicle is stocked with the following equipment:
     - Fibre splicing kit and fusion splicer
     - Copper termination tools (punch-down tool, RJ11/RJ45 dies)
     - Cable toner and probe
     - Optical power meter and light source
     - Cable tester (continuity and length)
     - Ladder (6 ft and 12 ft)
     - Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): hard hat, safety glasses, hi-vis vest,
       gloves, steel-toed boots
     - First aid kit
     - Lockout/tagout kit
     - Spare connectors, patch leads, wall plates, and faceplate assemblies
     - Work order documentation and tablet or phone with Allied Henna Telecom
       field app installed

1.3  Check weather conditions and assess any site-specific hazards logged in
     the field app before departure.

1.4  Confirm your schedule with the customer at least 30 minutes before arrival.

1.5  Ensure your vehicle communication device and mobile phone are fully charged.


SECTION 2: ON-SITE ARRIVAL & SAFETY
--------------------------------------------------------------

2.1  Park in a safe, designated area. Do not block driveways or emergency access.

2.2  Before entering any premises, present your Allied Henna Telecom ID badge
     to the customer or site representative.

2.3  Conduct a visual site hazard assessment before beginning any work:
     - Identify overhead power lines
     - Check for unstable ground, wet surfaces, or confined space entry points
     - Note any signage indicating hazardous materials

2.4  Do not enter roof cavities, sub-floor spaces, or confined areas alone.
     Notify dispatch before entering confined spaces and confirm check-in/check-out
     times.

2.5  Apply lockout/tagout procedures on any electrical cabinets or MDF/IDF
     enclosures before working on live equipment panels.

2.6  Never work on live cables without confirming isolation with a voltage tester.


SECTION 3: BUILDING CONNECTION — FIBRE SERVICE
--------------------------------------------------------------

3.1  Locate the Network Termination Point (NTP) or the designated entry conduit
     as per the site plan in your work order.

3.2  Inspect the exterior conduit entry for damage, moisture ingress, or
     obstruction before threading cable.

3.3  Pull the drop fibre cable through the conduit using an approved pull cord.
     Do not exceed the cable's minimum bend radius (typically 30 mm for drop cable).

3.4  At the NTP/ONT mounting location:
     a. Fix the wall bracket securely into a load-bearing surface using
        appropriate anchors.
     b. Mount the ONT (Optical Network Terminal) unit level and upright.
     c. Route cable with adequate slack for servicing (minimum 1 m service loop).

3.5  Perform a fibre splice or SC/APC connector termination according to the
     Allied Henna Telecom Fibre Termination Standard (ref: AHT-FTS-003).

3.6  Clean all connectors with a fibre cleaning tool before mating. Never
     inspect a live fibre port with the naked eye.

3.7  Measure optical power at the ONT receive port using the optical power meter:
     - Acceptable receive level: -8 dBm to -27 dBm
     - If outside range, check for excessive bend, dirty connectors, or splice loss.

3.8  Power up the ONT and confirm link status LEDs are green before proceeding
     to customer equipment setup.


SECTION 4: BUILDING CONNECTION — COPPER SERVICE
--------------------------------------------------------------

4.1  Locate the existing or new Main Distribution Frame (MDF) or external
     junction box as indicated on the work order.

4.2  Inspect all copper pairs for continuity and insulation resistance using the
     cable tester before committing a pair to service.

4.3  Terminate the service pair at the MDF using a punch-down tool on IDC
     (Insulation Displacement Contact) blocks. Verify polarity: Tip (white/blue)
     to Pin 4, Ring (blue) to Pin 5 for POTS; follow DSL pair allocation table
     AHT-DSL-001 for broadband services.

4.4  Route internal cable neatly, secured with cable clips at no greater than
     300 mm intervals. Avoid running parallel to 240 V mains wiring for more
     than 1 m.

4.5  Test the completed circuit end-to-end with the cable toner and confirm
     with the customer's socket.

4.6  Record loop resistance, insulation resistance, and pair balance readings
     in the field app before closing the job.


SECTION 5: WIRELESS POINT-TO-POINT / FIXED WIRELESS CONNECTION
--------------------------------------------------------------

5.1  Confirm line-of-sight (LOS) between the customer premises and the nearest
     Allied Henna Telecom fixed wireless node using the LOS tool in the field app.

5.2  Select the mounting location for the Customer Premises Equipment (CPE)
     antenna — highest unobstructed point on the building exterior, clear of
     HVAC units and metallic structures.

5.3  Mount the antenna bracket securely to the fascia, wall, or roof mast.
     All roof penetrations must be sealed with weatherproof mastic.

5.4  Align the antenna using the signal strength indicator in the CPE web
     interface. Target RSSI: -60 dBm or better. Tighten all mounting bolts
     once alignment is confirmed.

5.5  Route the Ethernet cable from the CPE to the indoor Power over Ethernet
     (PoE) injector using weatherproof gland entry. Bond the cable screen to
     the building earth if instructed by the work order.

5.6  Confirm IP connectivity and run a throughput test via the field app.
     Minimum acceptable: 90% of provisioned speed.


SECTION 6: CUSTOMER EQUIPMENT & HANDOVER
--------------------------------------------------------------

6.1  Connect the Allied Henna Telecom-supplied router or modem to the NTP/ONT
     or CPE as applicable. Confirm broadband sync and IP address acquisition.

6.2  Test customer-facing services as specified in the work order (internet,
     VoIP, IPTV). Document all test results in the field app.

6.3  Label all equipment with the Allied Henna Telecom asset tag. Photograph
     all installed equipment and upload images to the work order before leaving.

6.4  Brief the customer on basic equipment operation, LED status indicators,
     and the Allied Henna Telecom support contact number (1800-AHT-HELP).

6.5  Obtain the customer's signature on the job completion form via the field
     app before leaving the premises.

6.6  Remove all waste cable, packaging, and offcuts from the site. Dispose of
     electronic waste at an approved Allied Henna Telecom depot recycling point.


SECTION 7: TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE
--------------------------------------------------------------

7.1  No ONT Link Light (Fibre)
     - Check ONT power supply and power outlet.
     - Inspect fibre cable for visible damage or tight bends.
     - Clean and re-seat the SC/APC connector.
     - Measure optical power at the ONT receive port. If no light detected,
       trace back to the splice point or distribution cabinet.
     - Escalate to the Network Operations Centre (NOC) if the issue is upstream
       of the NTP.

7.2  ONT Online but No Broadband Service
     - Confirm provisioning status with NOC via field app.
     - Restart the ONT (power cycle, wait 3 minutes).
     - Check ONT serial number matches the provisioned record in the field app.

7.3  Copper — No Dial Tone / No DSL Sync
     - Verify correct pair is jumperedthrough the MDF.
     - Test for line voltage: 48 V DC ± 6 V on-hook.
     - Test for foreign voltage or excessive noise with a butt set.
     - If noise or low voltage is detected, log a fault with the NOC for a
       line plant investigation.

7.4  Wireless CPE — Poor Signal / No Link
     - Confirm antenna alignment has not shifted (check for wind damage or
       loose mount bolts).
     - Check for new obstructions in the LOS path.
     - Re-run alignment procedure (Section 5.4).
     - If node-side issue is suspected, notify NOC for remote node diagnostics.

7.5  Router / Modem Not Connecting
     - Factory reset the device only if authorised in the work order.
     - Confirm correct VLAN and PPPoE credentials are configured.
     - Swap with a tested spare unit if available.
     - Escalate to Level 2 support if the issue persists after equipment swap.

7.6  Intermittent Service After Installation
     - Check all connector terminations for proper seating.
     - Inspect cable runs for pinch points or sharp bends.
     - Review ONT receive power over time via the field app diagnostic graph
       for optical fluctuation.
     - Log a follow-up visit if the fault cannot be reproduced on-site.


SECTION 8: DEALING WITH CREATURES ON SITE
--------------------------------------------------------------

Field technicians frequently access roof cavities, sub-floor spaces, wall
cavities, and outdoor equipment enclosures where animals may be present.
Follow these procedures to protect yourself and the animals.

8.1  General Precautions (All Creatures)
     - Before reaching into any dark space (cabinet, conduit, cavity), use
       a torch to inspect the area first.
     - Wear heavy-duty gloves when opening external enclosures or working in
       roof cavities.
     - Do not attempt to handle, capture, or harm any animal. If an animal
       blocks access, vacate the area calmly and contact dispatch.
     - Document the presence of any creature in the work order notes with a
       photograph if it can be taken safely.

8.2  Insects (Wasps, Bees, Spiders)
     - If a nest or significant spider infestation is found inside a cabinet
       or conduit entry point, do not disturb it.
     - Mark the hazard in the field app, notify dispatch, and do not proceed
       with work until a pest controller has cleared the site.
     - If stung or bitten, apply first aid from your kit. If you have a known
       allergy, use your EpiPen immediately and call emergency services (000).

8.3  Rodents (Rats, Mice)
     - Rodent activity (droppings, gnawed cable) must be photographed and
       logged. Gnawed cable must be replaced — do not tape and leave.
     - Wear gloves and a dust mask (P2 rating) when working in areas with
       rodent evidence, as droppings can carry disease.
     - Notify the customer and include a remediation note in the work order.

8.4  Snakes
     - Do not attempt to move or handle a snake under any circumstances.
     - Back away slowly and calmly. Alert the customer to keep others away
       from the area.
     - Contact a licensed wildlife handler or local council snake catcher
       via dispatch.
     - Do not re-enter the affected area until cleared by the wildlife handler.
     - Record the incident in the field app and complete an Allied Henna Telecom
       incident report form.

8.5  Possums, Birds, and Other Wildlife
     - Many native species are protected. Do not disturb nests, eggs, or young.
     - If an animal is nesting inside an enclosure required for the installation,
       contact dispatch. A wildlife relocation permit may be required before
       work can proceed.
     - If an animal appears injured, contact the local wildlife rescue line
       via dispatch for guidance.

8.6  Domestic Animals (Dogs, Cats)
     - Confirm with the customer that dogs are secured before entering the
       property, particularly rear yards and garages.
     - If a dog acts aggressively, withdraw to a safe position and ask the
       customer to restrain it before you proceed.
     - Do not enter a property where you cannot confirm the animal is secured.
