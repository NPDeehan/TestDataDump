Below are **structured, practical guidelines for dispute resolution in a shipping/logistics company**. They are written so they can be used internally by **operations, finance, and customer service teams**.

---

# Dispute Resolution Guidelines

## 1. Log and Categorize the Dispute

1.1 All customer disputes must be **logged immediately in the dispute management system** (or ticketing system).
1.2 Record the following information:

* Customer name
* Invoice number
* Shipment / booking number
* Dispute type (use dispute codes such as D01–D18 where applicable)
* Amount disputed
* Customer explanation
* Date dispute was received

1.3 Assign the correct **dispute category** to ensure the issue is routed to the correct team (operations, finance, sales, etc.).

---

# 2. Verify the Invoice Before Taking Action

2.1 Review the invoice against the following documentation:

* Customer contract or tariff
* Rate agreement
* Booking confirmation
* Bill of Lading (B/L)
* Operational records (gate-in/out, truck waiting times, etc.)
* System charge calculation

2.2 Confirm:

* The **charges were applied correctly**
* The **currency and exchange rate are correct**
* The **customer is the correct payer**

2.3 If the invoice is correct, prepare a **clear explanation for the customer with supporting documents**.

---

# 3. When to Contact Sales

Sales should be contacted in the following situations:

3.1 **Rate discrepancies**

* The rate charged does not match the **contracted rate or quotation**
* The customer claims a **special rate or commercial agreement**

3.2 **Commercial exceptions**

* Requests for **goodwill adjustments**
* Requests for **commercial credit or discounts**

3.3 **Contract interpretation**

* When the terms of a contract or quotation are unclear

Sales is responsible for:

* Confirming the **agreed commercial rate**
* Approving **rate adjustments**
* Communicating any commercial decisions.

---

# 4. When to Request an Invoice Reversal

An **invoice reversal** should only be requested when the issued invoice **cannot be corrected through adjustment or credit memo alone**.

### Reasons for Invoice Reversal

4.1 The **wrong payer** was assigned.
4.2 The invoice was issued with **incorrect charges or major calculation errors**.
4.3 The **incorrect currency or exchange rate** was used.
4.4 The invoice was generated **for the wrong shipment or booking**.
4.5 The invoice was issued **before operational data was finalized**.
4.6 The invoice includes **charges that should not have been billed at all**.

---

# 5. How to Request an Invoice Reversal

5.1 Verify internally that a reversal is required and **cannot be solved with a credit note**.

5.2 Submit a reversal request to the **finance/billing team** including:

* Invoice number
* Shipment or booking number
* Reason for reversal
* Dispute type code
* Supporting documentation
* Approval from Sales (if rate-related)

5.3 Clearly indicate:

* Whether the invoice should be **reissued**
* Whether the charges should be **removed entirely**

5.4 Once reversed:

* Issue a **corrected invoice**
* Inform the customer promptly.

---

# 6. Handling Operational Charge Disputes

Operational disputes typically involve:

* Truck waiting time
* Haulage costs
* Equipment cleaning fees
* Port handling charges
* Detention or demurrage

6.1 Validate the charges against:

* Terminal records
* Trucking reports
* Equipment logs
* Time stamps

6.2 If the charge is valid, provide **evidence to the customer**.

6.3 If the charge is incorrect, request:

* **Charge correction**
* **Credit note or invoice reversal**

---

# 7. Handling “Invoice Already Paid” Claims

7.1 Verify payment in the accounting system.
7.2 Check:

* Payment reference
* Payment date
* Amount paid
* Currency

7.3 If payment is confirmed:

* Close the dispute
* Update the invoice status.

7.4 If payment cannot be located:

* Request **proof of payment** from the customer.

---

# 8. Handling Demurrage / Detention Disputes

8.1 Confirm the **free time allowed under the contract**.
8.2 Review container movement records.
8.3 Validate:

* Gate-in / gate-out times
* Empty return dates
* Terminal data

8.4 If an error exists, request adjustment through **operations or terminal coordination**.

---

# 9. Communication with the Customer

9.1 Acknowledge receipt of the dispute **within 1 business day**.
9.2 Provide an **expected resolution timeframe**.
9.3 Maintain clear communication during investigation.
9.4 Always provide **documentation when rejecting a dispute**.

---

# 10. Escalation Procedure

Escalate the dispute if:

* Resolution takes longer than **10 business days**
* The dispute amount exceeds **internal approval limits**
* There is **disagreement between departments**

Escalation levels:

1. Billing / Dispute Specialist
2. Finance Manager
3. Sales Manager / Commercial Manager
4. Senior Management

---

# 11. Closing the Dispute

A dispute can be closed when:

* The customer **accepts the invoice**
* A **credit note or invoice reversal has been issued**
* Payment has been received

Ensure:

* The dispute record is updated
* Supporting documents are attached
* Final communication is sent to the customer.
