# Mission Brief — MCRN Rocinante
**Classification:** Crew Access  
**Mission Designation:** Operation Pale Horizon  
**Departure:** 2351-09-14  
**Estimated Transit Duration:** 187 days  

---

## Destination

**System:** Kepler-442  
**Target Body:** Kepler-442c (provisional designation: *Amaranth*)  
**Distance from Sol:** 1,194 light-years (relay-assisted transit via Laconia Gate)  
**Orbital Period:** 112 standard days  
**Surface Gravity:** 0.91g  
**Atmosphere:** Nitrogen-oxygen mix, breathable with minor filtration  

Amaranth was flagged by long-range survey probes in 2349 as a candidate for human habitation. High-resolution scans have revealed stable landmasses, liquid surface water, and anomalous structural formations of unknown origin in the northern equatorial region. The nature of these formations is the primary driver of this mission.

## Mission Objectives

### Primary
1. **Survey the anomalous formations** in the northern equatorial zone — document structure, composition, and origin. Determine whether formations are geological or artificial.
2. **Conduct full atmospheric and biosphere assessment** of Amaranth's surface across a minimum of three landing zones.
3. **Collect and preserve samples** — soil, water, atmosphere, and biological material if present — for return to the Laconia Science Directorate.

### Secondary
4. **Establish a surface beacon array** at the primary landing site to support any follow-on missions.
5. **Assess habitability** for long-duration human presence, including water extraction, radiation shielding requirements, and local resource availability.

### Contingency
6. **If artificial origin of formations is confirmed**, the crew is authorised to conduct a limited non-invasive interior survey. Under no circumstances are crew members to remove, damage, or transmit data on formation interiors without direct authorisation from command.
